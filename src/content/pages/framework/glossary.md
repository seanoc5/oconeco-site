# Glossary of Novel Constructs

OconEco uses precise terminology for concepts that mainstream economics either elides or names differently. This glossary defines all 30 novel constructs in a single place, with cross-references between related terms.

## Sample entries (placeholder)

### Commonwealth (CW)
The total wealth owned by the people of a place — the left-hand side of the FAND balance sheet. Computed from GNI per capita and working-age population via an annuity construction. Distinguished from Net Worth (NW), which adds gross cross-border position.
*Related:* Net Worth, PMT annuity.

### Hohfeldian classification
The legal-theory framework (Wesley Newcomb Hohfeld, 1913) that distinguishes *claim-rights* (correlative to duties) from *liberties / privileges* (correlative to no-rights). FAND uses Hohfeld to classify what can be collateralized (claim-rights) vs what can only be used (substantive rights).
*Related:* Claim-Rights, Substantive Rights, IS-Branch Principle.

### IS-Branch Principle
Institutional infrastructure — rule of law, trust in courts, social cohesion — that conventional methodologies *assume* but do not *measure*. FAND measures it explicitly under Substantive Rights / Trust Infrastructure.
*Related:* SI, SR, Deferred Maintenance.

### Commonwealth-as-wēle
The Old English root of "wealth" (*wēle*) connotes well-being and welfare, not just possessions. FAND's Commonwealth carries that older meaning: what the people of a place have, in the sense of what makes them well-off, not only what they can sell.
*Related:* Commonwealth.

### Synaptic Cleft
The gap between conventional national accounts and the FAND balance sheet — where the substantive-rights side, omitted by SNA-93/SNA-2008, makes the difference between an income-flow story (GDP) and a wealth-stock story (CW).
*Related:* MEP 1995, Changing Wealth of Nations.
