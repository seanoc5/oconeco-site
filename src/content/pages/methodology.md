# Methodology

Two features anchor the methodology section: the **Operational Guidelines** (how we handle specific methodological domains) and **NUSAP** (how much to trust each number). Neither is front-page material, but they signal to institutional reviewers that this is peer-grade work.

## The four sub-pages

- **[Operational Guidelines (OG-001..OG-010)](/methodology/og/)** — 10 guidelines codifying methodological domains. Accordion: one-line summary visible, full text on expand.
- **[NUSAP — data quality](/methodology/nusap/)** — the Funtowicz & Ravetz framework, displayed progressively on every value (hover → spread; click → assessment + pedigree).
- **[Source agency directory](/methodology/sources/)** — every number links to its source agency, table, year, and URL. This is the directory of sources we cherry-pick from.
- **[Replication package](/methodology/replication/)** — the 12-stage Python pipeline, the SHA-256 audit trail, and the public GitHub or gated download.

## Why methodology gets its own section

Most data portals show you a number without telling you how much to trust it. FAND shows the quality metadata alongside every value. That is a differentiator institutional reviewers will notice — and a forcing function on us to not publish numbers we cannot defend.
