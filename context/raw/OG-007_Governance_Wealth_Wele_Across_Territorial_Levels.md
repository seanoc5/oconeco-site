# OG-007: Governance, Wealth, and Wēle Across Territorial Levels

**Operational Guideline — Session 25, 21 March 2026**

---

## 1. Scope and Purpose

This guideline establishes the conceptual architecture connecting Knight's Wheel of Wealth (the three-spoke flow identity: Production, Income, Expenditure) to the Commonwealth balance sheet (the stock of wealth and well-being) across US territorial levels TL1 (national), TL2 (states), and TL3 (counties). It addresses three linked problems:

1. **Flow to stock**: The three spokes measure income flows; the balance sheet measures wealth stocks. The CW capitalization formula converts flows to stocks, but the *choice* of which flow to capitalize is a governance question, not a measurement question.

2. **Place to people**: The asset side of the balance sheet measures productive capacity *of a place* (territorial); Net Worth after liabilities approximates wēle *for those who reside* in that place (residential). Cross-border adjustments at each territorial level bridge these two concepts.

3. **Powers across levels**: The Hohfeldian structure of rights, powers, and institutional capacity changes at each territorial boundary — and that structure determines what SI means, what liabilities recognize, and ultimately what wēle captures at each level.

---

## 2. The Fundamental Identity and Why It Is Not Enough

### 2.1 Knight's Wheel: GDP ≡ GNI ≡ GDE

The three spokes of Knight's Wheel are identically equal at every geographic level. They cannot diverge. What changes across territorial levels is the meaning and measurability of the cross-border adjustment terms that reconcile them:

| Level | "Cross-border" means | Measured by BEA? |
|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| TL1 (national) | International | Fully (ITA, BoP) |
| TL2 (state) | International + inter-state | Partially (SAINC91 for earnings; property income derivable; trade balance not measured) |
| TL3 (county) | International + inter-state + inter-county | Minimally (PI by residence; GDP by production; no explicit adjustment published) |

### 2.2 Why Flows Are Necessary but Insufficient

The three spokes tell us how much income is generated (Production), how much accrues to residents (Income), and how much residents consume (Expenditure) in a given year. But wēle — "a source of prosperity, well-being, joy" — is not a flow. It is a stock: the accumulated capacity to sustain well-being over time, net of obligations that diminish it.

The CW formula capitalizes an income flow into a wealth stock:

```
CW_pc = PMT × [(1.015^n − 1) / 0.015]
where PMT = Income_pc × Working_Pct_Pop, n = Remaining_Work_Yrs
```

This formula is spoke-agnostic — it accepts any income concept. But the *choice* of income concept embeds an assumption about which boundary's cross-border adjustments are appropriate, which in turn embeds an assumption about whose productive capacity we are measuring and for whom.

## 3. The Balance Sheet: Place versus People

### 3.1 Asset Side = Productive Capacity of a Place (Territorial)

The asset side of the Commonwealth balance sheet measures what a place can produce:

- **PA (Produced Assets)**: Physical capital located in this territory — factories, infrastructure, housing, equipment. Located where it stands.
- **NC (Natural Capital)**: Resources in the ground, on the land, in the water. Irreducibly territorial — the oil is where the oil is.
- **HR (Human Resources)**: Human capital of the workforce that operates in or is associated with this territory. Straddles the territorial/residential boundary (see §3.3).
- **SI (Social Infrastructure)**: The institutional capacity that operates at this level — courts, schools, regulatory apparatus, social trust, community organizations. Place-specific but not purely territorial (see §5).

### 3.2 Liability Side → Net Worth = Wēle for Residents (Residential)

The liability side recognizes obligations and structural failures that diminish the well-being of *residents*:

- **L_Sovereign**: Government debt that falls on residents as current and future taxpayers.
- **L_Carbon**: Environmental burden borne by residents and their descendants — CO₂ accumulated, air quality degraded, climate risk imposed.
- **L_Gender**: Structural exclusion that prevents residents from realizing their productive potential on the basis of sex.
- **L_Distribution**: Inequality among residents that represents unrealized potential — the Atkinson index captures the welfare loss from unequal distribution.

**Net Worth = CW − L_INTER − L_INTRA ≈ wēle**

The liabilities are "accounting fictions" in the sense that they are not SNA transactions — no market exchange created them. They are recognitions of Hohfeldian power imbalances (Pair III: power/liability) that produce real but unmeasured economic consequences. They capitalize in Q (physical units) before P (valuation) — rule H-4 from the Liability Framework Think Piece — because the physical obligation exists regardless of whether anyone has assigned a price.

### 3.3 The Bridge: Cross-Border Adjustments

The cross-border adjustments at each territorial level are the mechanism that converts the asset side's territorial reality into the residence-based flows that, when capitalized and liability-adjusted, yield wēle:

- **Production spoke (GDP)** activates the asset side: how much productive capacity was used this year *in this place*.
- **Income spoke (GNI/PI)** measures what accrues *to residents of this place* — adjusting for factor income that flows across the boundary in both directions.
- **Expenditure spoke (PCE)** measures what residents *actually consume* — the closest behavioral proxy for wēle from the flow side.

The wider the gap between GDP and PI at any level, the larger the cross-border flows, and the more the governance structure (Hohfeldian allocation of powers across levels) matters for determining who benefits from a place's productive capacity.

## 4. Sovereignty Does Not Flow Downward in the American System

### 4.1 The Colonial Origins of Property Rights

The standard hierarchy — TL1 holds sovereign powers, delegates to TL2, which delegates to TL3 — is a European unitary-state model that does not describe the American system. The actual historical sequence is more complex and runs in multiple directions.

**Property rights in colonial America were established locally before any state or national authority existed.** Land grants, recording systems, fee simple ownership, contract enforcement, and dispute resolution were colonial and often sub-colonial operations. Virginia's counties date from 1634. New England towns were self-governing before the colonial charter was formalized. The parish system in the South and the township system in New England administered property, collected taxes, maintained roads, and adjudicated disputes as operational governance units.

When independence severed the Crown connection, sovereignty did not automatically vest in states as pre-existing entities. It was constructed through the Articles of Confederation and then the Constitutional compact. The states did not receive powers from the federal government; they pooled certain enumerated powers upward. The 10th Amendment confirms this: powers not delegated to the United States are reserved to the states or to the people.

### 4.2 Dillon's Rule and Its Historical Limits

Dillon's Rule (1868) holds that municipalities and counties are creatures of the state, possessing only those powers explicitly granted by the state legislature. This maps cleanly onto the western territorial experience: federal territory → state admission → county creation. In Iowa, where Judge Dillon sat, the sequence was indeed top-down.

But applying Dillon's Rule retrospectively to the original states reverses the actual historical sequence. Virginia's counties, Massachusetts' towns, and Pennsylvania's boroughs preceded the state constitutions that Dillon's Rule treats as their source of authority. Local governance in the original colonies was not delegated from above — it was the operational reality from which higher-level authority was later constructed.

Even for western states, the reality was more complex: mining camps, ranching communities, and indigenous governance structures preceded federal territorial organization. The federal government's authority over territories derived from treaties, conquest, and doctrines whose legitimacy is contested.

**Consequence for SI**: Institutional capacity at TL3 is not a diminished version of TL2 institutional capacity, any more than TL2 is a diminished version of TL1. In the American system, local institutional capacity is historically prior in many jurisdictions, and the governance optimization problem — which Hohfeldian powers should sit at which level — runs in all directions simultaneously.

### 4.3 Census Recognition: Counties as Units of Analysis

The US Census Bureau's historical practice of estimating "true value" of property county by county — not state by state — provides empirical evidence that the county is an analytically meaningful unit, not merely an administrative subdivision. County-level assessment reflects local property markets, local assessment practices, and local institutional capacity. The county is where property rights are *operationally realized*: deeds recorded, assessments made, taxes collected, disputes adjudicated.

This is Smith's missing book made concrete. Property rights as legislated (state level) are not the same as property rights as administered (county level). The gap between legislation and administration is filled by local institutional capacity — and that capacity varies enormously across counties and is measurable through the assessment practices that the Census has tracked since the 19th century.

## 5. SI Across Territorial Levels: Not Nested, Orthogonal

### 5.1 Smith's Incomplete Triangle

Adam Smith intended a three-part philosophical architecture:

1. **The Theory of Moral Sentiments** (1759): The psychological foundation — sympathy, the impartial spectator, the sense of justice as natural to human social life.
2. **The Wealth of Nations** (1776): The economic system built on that foundation — but presupposing a functioning institutional order without fully theorizing it.
3. **Lectures on Jurisprudence** (the never-completed third work): How legal institutions — property, contract, the administration of justice — convert the moral capacity described in Moral Sentiments into the economic outcomes described in Wealth of Nations.

**SI is the balance-sheet proxy for Smith's missing book.** It sits between the asset side (productive capacity, which Wealth of Nations theorizes) and the liability side (failures of justice, which Law and Justice would have theorized). The Moral Sentiments layer is deeper still — it is the pre-institutional social capacity that enables institutions to form and function.

When Chetty finds that a child's zip code predicts their economic trajectory, he is measuring neighborhood-level Moral Sentiments outcomes: social trust, community expectations, implicit norms about what is possible. These flow through institutional channels (schools, policing, local governance) to produce economic results that SI captures as a balance-sheet residual.

### 5.2 SI at Each Level Captures Different Institutional Reality

SI = CW − HR − PA − NC (the balance-sheet residual at each level).

**This residual measures different things at different levels because the institutional capacity at each level is orthogonal, not nested:**

| Level | SI captures | Examples |
|-------|------------|----------|
| TL1 (federal) | Institutional capacity created by the constitutional compact — powers the states pooled upward | Monetary policy, treaty-making, interstate commerce regulation, civil rights framework, federal courts, military, social insurance (SS/Medicare) |
| TL2 (state) | Original and retained institutional capacity — powers that predate or were reserved from the federal level | Property law, education systems, police powers, state courts, land use regulation, Medicaid administration, state tax policy |
| TL3 (county) | Operational institutional capacity — the administration of rights, often historically prior to state authority | Property assessment, deed recording, local courts, school districts, zoning, local infrastructure, public health, emergency services |

National SI ≠ Σ(state SI). State SI ≠ Σ(county SI). Each level has institutional capacity that is emergent at that level and does not decompose into components at lower levels. Federal monetary policy does not apportion to states. Massachusetts' 400-year institutional accumulation does not decompose into county components.

### 5.3 The Synaptic Cleft Revisited

SI is the synaptic cleft between:
- **Pre-synaptic** (asset side): A place's productive capacity — what it *could* produce.
- **Post-synaptic** (NW/wēle side): What residents *actually experience* — productive capacity minus the liabilities that diminish it.

The signal that crosses the cleft is mediated by institutions operating at each level. Excitatory signals (social trust, network effects, institutional competence) increase the conversion rate from potential to realization. Inhibitory signals (discriminatory zoning, captured regulation, institutional decay) decrease it.

The width of the cleft — the gap between potential and realization — is what SI measures. And the governance structure (which Hohfeldian powers sit at which level) determines which institutions mediate the signal at each territorial boundary.

## 6. Liabilities Transform Across Levels

### 6.1 Same Concept, Different Hohfeldian Standing

Each liability exists at every level, but the Hohfeldian structure (who holds power, who bears subjection) changes:

**L_Sovereign:**
- TL1: Federal debt — backed by money-printing and universal taxing authority. Pair III: Congress holds power; all residents and future generations in liability.
- TL2: State debt — no monetary authority, balanced-budget requirements in most states, but bond-issuing and taxing power. Pair III: state legislature holds power; state residents in liability. Different constitutional standing than federal debt.
- TL3: County debt — exists at the pleasure of state legislature. Counties can be dissolved, merged, restructured (Detroit 2013 made this concrete). Pair III: county commission holds delegated power; county residents in liability — but the power is itself subject to state Pair III.

**L_Carbon:**
- TL1: National atmospheric externality — tonnes of CO₂ owed to the global commons and future generations. Federal regulatory choices (EPA, energy policy) exercise Pair III power.
- TL2: State regulatory variation — California's CARB vs Texas's permitting. Independent exercises of original Pair III power over environmental standards within state boundaries.
- TL3: County-level exposure — EPA FLIGHT facility emissions, local air quality, flood risk. The county is often in Pair III *liability* (subjection): it bears the health and environmental consequences without having held the power to prevent the emissions. The firm and the state/federal regulatory framework exercised the power; the county's residents experience the consequences.

**L_Gender / L_Distribution:**
- TL1: National structural exclusion — aggregate gender earnings gap, national Atkinson index. Reflects federal civil rights framework (Pair IV immunities post-14th Amendment) interacting with national labor market structure.
- TL2: State-level variation — Jim Crow was state law exercising original Pair III power. The 14th Amendment was a constitutional reallocation of power (creating individual Pair IV immunities against state action). Current L_Gender and L_Distribution variation across states reflects independent sovereign choices about labor law, education, healthcare, social provision.
- TL3: Neighborhood effects — Chetty's finding that zip code predicts trajectory. County-level L_INTRA reflects local institutional choices (school quality, policing, zoning, historical redlining) that operate through Pairs II–IV. Most directly measurable, most directly actionable, most directly tied to the Smith–Moral Sentiments layer of social trust and community expectations.

### 6.2 The Accounting Fictions Are More Grounded at Lower Levels

A counterintuitive finding: the "accounting fictions" on the liability side may be *less* fictional at TL3 than at TL1. National L_Carbon is a global atmospheric abstraction. County L_Carbon is the asthma rate in the neighborhoods downwind of the refinery. National L_Distribution is an inequality index. County L_Distribution is the school funding disparity between adjacent districts. National L_Gender is a labor force participation gap. County L_Gender is the availability or absence of childcare infrastructure.

The more local the level, the more directly the liability connects to lived experience — to wēle in the original Middle English sense.

## 7. Operationalization: What to Build

### 7.1 State Level (TL2)

**Three income variants for CW capitalization:**
- GDP_pc (Production spoke): SAGDP2. Measures asset-side activation.
- GNI_pc (Income spoke): State GNI v2 = GDP + international NFI + SAINC91 residence adjustment + property income adjustment. Measures what accrues to residents.
- PCE_pc (Expenditure spoke): SAEXP1. Measures what residents consume. To fetch.

**Three CW variants:**
- CW_gdp_pc → asset-side-weighted wealth
- CW_gni_pc → residence-adjusted wealth
- CW_pce_pc → consumption-weighted wealth

**SI derivation at state level:** SI = CW − HR − PA − NC, where each component is state-level. Captures state-level institutional capacity (property law, education, regulation, courts).

**Liabilities at state level:** L_Sovereign (state debt, Census of Governments), L_Carbon (EPA FLIGHT aggregated to state), L_Gender (ACS income by sex), L_Distribution (ACS Gini/Atkinson by state).

**Net Worth = CW − L_INTER − L_INTRA ≈ state-level wēle for residents.**

### 7.2 County Level (TL3)

**Two-and-a-half income variants:**
- GDP_pc / GTP_pc (Production spoke): CAGDP2 and GTP from QCEW×κ pipeline. GTP corrects HQ bias.
- PI_pc (Income spoke proxy): CAINC1. Already residence-based — BEA's internal residence adjustment is embedded. Best available county GNI proxy.
- PCE_pc: Not available at county level. Deferred.

**CW variants:**
- CW_gdp_pc, CW_gtp_pc → asset-side-weighted wealth (two versions)
- CW_pi_pc → residence-adjusted wealth

**SI derivation at county level:** SI = CW − HR − PA − NC. Captures county-level operational institutional capacity (property assessment, schools, local courts, zoning, public services). This is the first-ever subnational institutional capital measurement and connects to the Census "true value" tradition.

**Liabilities at county level:** L_Sovereign (county debt, Census of Governments biennial), L_Carbon (EPA FLIGHT facility-level), L_Gender (ACS income by sex at county level), L_Distribution (ACS Gini at county level; CHR health disparities).

**Net Worth = CW − L_INTER − L_INTRA ≈ county-level wēle for residents.**

### 7.3 The Diagnostic: Cross-Border Residuals

At each level, the gap between income variants is the implicit cross-border adjustment:
- GDP − GNI = net factor income outflow (what the place produces but residents don't receive)
- GDP − PCE = net saving + net trade balance (what the place produces but residents don't consume)
- GNI − PCE = saving rate (what residents earn but don't consume)

These residuals are as analytically valuable as the levels themselves. A county where GDP >> PI is a net exporter of factor income (HQ bias, commuter outflow). A state where GNI >> PCE has high saving or net trade surplus. The cross-border residuals diagnose the *governance structure* — which Hohfeldian powers are active at each boundary.

## 9. Telescoping Cross-Territorial Investment Positions

### 9.1 The Nesting Principle

The balance sheet at each territorial level carries a cross-border position that decomposes into layers, each netting to zero when summed to the parent level:

**TL1 (National):** A single Net International Investment Position (NIIP) entry — on the asset side if the nation is a net creditor, on the liability side if a net debtor. This is what NC_CrossBorder_FAND.xlsx computes from IMF IIP data. For the USA in 2020: NIIP = -$14.6 trillion (liability side).

**TL2 (State):** Two layers:
- **Layer 1 — International apportionment:** Each state's share of the national NIIP, allocated via the same weights used in State GNI v1 (AMNE employment for inward FDI, property income for portfolio/other, GDP share for outward FDI and CE). By construction, the sum of state international shares equals the national NIIP exactly.
- **Layer 2 — Inter-state positions:** Net claims between states arising from cross-border earnings, property income, and investment flows. These are the *stock equivalent* of the flows observed in SAINC91 and SAINC40. By construction, the sum of all inter-state positions equals zero at TL1 — every state's inter-state asset is another state's inter-state liability.

For any state *s*:
```
Total_Cross_Border_s = International_NIIP_share_s + Inter_State_Position_s
where Sigma_s(Inter_State_Position_s) = 0 (exact, by construction)
```

**TL3 (County):** Three layers:
- **Layer 1 — International apportionment:** Each county's share of the national NIIP (cascaded from TL2 via population or GDP weight).
- **Layer 2 — Inter-state apportionment:** Each county inherits its state's inter-state position, allocated within-state by county GDP or PI share.
- **Layer 3 — Inter-county positions:** Net claims between counties within the same state, arising from commuter earnings, local property income flows, and establishment-vs-HQ booking. By construction, these sum to zero within each state.

For any county *c* in state *s*:
```
Total_Cross_Border_c = International_NIIP_share_c + Inter_State_share_c + Inter_County_Position_c
where Sigma_{c in s}(Inter_County_Position_c) = 0 for every state s (exact)
```

### 9.2 Stocks vs. Flows: The Capitalization Bridge

A critical distinction: the IMF IIP provides *observed stock positions* at TL1. At TL2 and TL3, we have primarily *flow observations* (SAINC91 earnings adjustments, SAINC40 property income, GTP/GDP gaps). The stock positions at subnational levels must be *derived* from persistent flow differentials, not observed directly.

The capitalization approach mirrors the CW annuity framework: if a state persistently receives net inter-state earnings inflows of $X per year, the imputed stock position is X multiplied by an appropriate annuity or perpetuity factor. This is conceptually defensible — a New Jersey resident who commutes to New York for 25 remaining work years has effectively "invested" their human capital across a state border, and the PV of that cross-border income stream is a legitimate balance-sheet stock.

The pedigree consequence: TL1 positions are HIGH confidence (directly observed in IMF IIP). TL2 positions are MEDIUM confidence (derived from BEA flow data with well-understood allocation weights). TL3 positions are LOW confidence but structurally grounded (inferred from GDP/PI gaps, GTP/GDP ratios, and commuting pattern proxies like LODES/LEHD).

### 9.3 The Balance-Sheet Entry Rule (generalized)

At each level, the total cross-border position enters the balance sheet following the same sign convention as the national NIIP:
- If `Total_Cross_Border >= 0` for unit *u* → asset side (claim-right on external productive capacity)
- If `Total_Cross_Border < 0` for unit *u* → liability side (duty to external claimants on local productive capacity)

This preserves the fundamental distinction: the **asset side** measures productive capacity of a *place* (territorial); the **liability side** adjusts for claims, and **Net Worth** approximates wele for those who *reside* in that place (residential). The cross-border entries at each level are precisely the bridge between territorial and residential perspectives.

### 9.4 Practical Ambition and Staging

Whether we achieve all three layers at all three levels before June 2026 is a practical question, not a conceptual one. The architecture should be complete in specification even where data gaps force estimation or deferral:

- **Immediate (Sessions 26-27):** State GNI v2 incorporating SAINC91 gives us the TL2 flow decomposition. Capitalizing those flows to imputed stock positions completes TL2 Layer 2.
- **Near-term (Phase 4):** Compute CW_gni_pc at state level using GNI_pc from v2. This is the residence-adjusted wealth measure at TL2.
- **Deferred:** TL3 Layer 3 (inter-county positions) awaits LEHD/LODES commuting data or can be approximated as GTP − PI residuals. Lower priority given the magnitude asymmetry (inter-county flows are smaller relative to county GDP than inter-state flows are relative to state GDP).
