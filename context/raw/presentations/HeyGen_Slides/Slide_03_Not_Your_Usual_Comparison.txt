Pick one administrative level — say TL2 — and compare all 2,500 regions as if they were equivalent. But a German Kreis has 80,000 people while a Nigerian State has 10 million. Treating them as equal units means Germany gets 401 'votes' in any analysis while Nigeria gets 37. Rich countries with fine-grained statistical systems dominate the sample.
We use the finest grain available per country, then assign quintiles by population weight. Each quintile contains approximately 20% of world population — not 20% of units. A unit with 10 million people carries 125 times the weight of one with 80,000.
The foot of this slide proves the quintiles are about equal in population. density quintiles range from 19% to 20% of world population. Per-capita economy quintiles range from 19% to 21— slightly wider because many leaves near the Q1/Q2 boundary share very similar per-capita values and can't be split exactly.
This means the lumpiness in unit counts we see on the next slides is a FINDING about the world's data infrastructure, not an artifact of our method. The population balance is locked in; what varies is how many administrative containers each country uses to hold its share of humanity.
