This is where the two frameworks diverge most sharply, both in conception and in results.

CWoN estimates Human Capital bottom-up as a stand-alone component — the present value of future lifetime earnings, using Jorgenson-Fraumeni methodology. FAND estimates Human Resources top-down — it is one piece of a residual-bearing identity. CW equals PA plus NC plus HR plus SI, where SI is the unexplained residual.

FAND's HR is fitted via OLS regression of log residual wealth on Mean Years of Schooling, Life Expectancy capped at 65, and a time trend. The cap at 65 is structurally grounded: post-retirement longevity is endogenous to wealth and does not represent additional productive human capital.

CWoN's HC typically dominates their total wealth estimate — often 60 to 70 percent. FAND's HR is more modest because it coexists with SI, the institutional-quality residual. The non-monotonic deflator finding from earlier means CWoN's "real" HC time paths are unreliable for trend analysis. FAND's HR, derived from a stable regression specification, produces smooth, interpretable trends.
