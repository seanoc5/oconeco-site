This presentation walks through Appendix E of the OconEco advocacy package. It compares two frameworks for measuring national wealth: FAND, the First Agentic Navigator for Development, and CWoN, the World Bank's Changing Wealth of Nations.

Both frameworks agree that understanding wealth requires decomposing value into price and quantity. V equals Q times P. But they make fundamentally different choices about how to handle prices — and those choices cascade through every component of the balance sheet.

FAND prefers to admit that index number problems are intractable. Only nominal values travel hierarchically. CWoN, like most mainstream indicator sets, presumes money is a veil you strip away to see what is "real." It deflates everything into constant dollars using component-specific chained deflators.

This presentation shows why that matters — with data from ten countries spanning the development spectrum.
