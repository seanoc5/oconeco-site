The full FAND balance sheet for 2020, constant 2020 dollars per capita. The identity CW equals PA plus NC plus HR plus SI holds to machine precision in nominal terms.

The pattern is clear. Advanced economies have SI — Savings and Institutional Quality — of 28 to 50 percent of Comprehensive Wealth. This reflects strong institutions, rule of law, social cohesion, and governance quality.

China has crossed into positive SI territory at 23.7%, consistent with its graduation trajectory from lower-middle to upper-middle income.

India and Indonesia show negative SI — minus 14.6% and minus 16.5% respectively. This means their institutional quality is a drag on wealth, not a contributor. The Deferred Maintenance column shows non-zero values for these countries: $972 and $1,067 per capita. DM is the liability side of the SI decomposition — it appears when SI falls below 20% of CW.

Nigeria sits just barely positive at 4.7%, consistent with resource-dependent governance challenges.

CWoN has no equivalent of SI. Because CWoN sums components bottom-up with no residual, it cannot identify institutional drag. FAND's residual-bearing identity is what makes SI visible and measurable.
